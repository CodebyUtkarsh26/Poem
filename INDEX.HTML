One Day, Maybe

Every morning I wake with a whisper of hope,

That today might be the day you finally know.

I carry a smile, but it hides the ache,

Of a heart that beats only for your sake.


I see you in school, so close yet far,

And wonder what flaw keeps you from my heart’s star.

I ask myself, with eyes that search the sky,

What is it in me that makes even God deny?


You’re angry with me, I can feel it still,

But my love for you bends to no storm or will.

It stands like a tree through seasons of pain,

Rooted in faith, through sunshine and rain.


So many times, I whisper, “Go talk to her today,”

But the moment I see you, my words fade away.

My mind goes silent, my courage hides,

Like a wave that retreats from the shore it slides.


Old chats flash back like a thunderstorm’s cry,

Each word a raindrop that clouds my eye.

But I hold the tears and clench back tight,

Telling myself, “One day, she’ll see the light.”


I love you today, like I did from the start,

With no expectations, just a loyal heart.

Even if you don’t feel the same way yet,

I’ll wait, I’ll love — without regret.


Because maybe one day, you'll turn around,

And find in me what others never found.

Till then I’ll live, with this quiet flame,

Hoping one day… you’ll call out my name.


— For Aadya, with all my heart
